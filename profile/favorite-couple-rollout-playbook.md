# Favorite Couple — Country Rollout Playbook

How a country goes from nothing to a live, sponsored competition — and how that repeats into a templated process. The guiding rule: **no country goes live without a secured prize, and qualification is something a couple earns, never something handed out for free.**

---

## 1. Operating Principle

Two things run on different clocks:

- **Pre-registration is always-on, everywhere.** A couple in any country can join the waitlist at any time. This continuously collects demand signal and becomes the sponsor pitch ("you'd be launching to 600 couples already waiting in your market").
- **A country only *activates* once its sponsor is locked.** Activation opens the qualification window and starts voting. Until then the country sits in pre-registration, warm but not live.

This keeps you disciplined (never launch without a prize) while never stopping demand collection.

---

## 2. The Per-Country Phases

### Phase 0 — Pre-registration (continuous)
- Couples join the country waitlist, create a profile, and start building their story.
- Each couple immediately receives a **unique referral code** (see §3).
- Goal in this phase: keep couples warm and *active*, not idle. They build profiles, post their story, and invite other couples — which is exactly the behavior you want anyway.
- This phase has no end date; it runs until the country activates.

### Phase 1 — Sponsor secured (the activation gate)
- A sponsor commits the first prize for that country (cash, a vacation, products, or a title).
- **Fallback (must be defined up front):** if no sponsor is secured within a set window (e.g. 60 days from reaching a minimum waitlist size), launch with a modest **company-funded starter prize** rather than letting the country stall indefinitely. A stalled country with no path to launch leaks couples.
- Once the prize is confirmed, announce it — the sponsor reveal is a launch moment in itself (§6).

### Phase 2 — Qualification window (the knockout)
- Opens on activation, runs for a fixed window (e.g. 14 days).
- Every waitlisted couple, and any new couple, qualifies for the live competition by **one of the paths in §3**.
- Couples that don't qualify by the deadline don't enter this round (they stay on the waitlist for next month).
- **Fairness rule:** the live competition does not begin until this window *closes*. Everyone who qualifies — by referral, votes, or founder status — enters the live round together, on a level footing. No one is voting in the "real" competition while others are still scrambling to qualify.

### Phase 3 — Live competition (monthly)
- All qualified couples compete. Free daily votes, paid votes, and 5× boosts run as specified in the build spec.
- The prize (and any company-set growing-prize tiers) is displayed throughout.
- Social channels run daily during this phase (§6).

### Phase 4 — Winner & payout
- Winner declared at period end.
- Identity verification, then payout (manual in early stage).
- Public winner announcement + sponsor thank-you — this is also your best content and your proof for the *next* sponsor.

### Phase 5 — Retain & repeat
- The country now runs monthly. Non-winning couples roll forward; new couples keep pre-registering and qualifying.
- Top performers feed the future regional/continental tiers (out of MVP scope, but the data is captured).
- Once a country is self-sustaining, it becomes the **template** for the next country.

---

## 3. How a Couple Qualifies (three paths)

A couple enters the live competition by **any one** of these. Multiple paths exist so no couple is ever stuck.

### Path A — Referral (the primary, preferred path)
- A couple qualifies by getting **one other couple** to register and verify using their referral code.
- **The chain:** once the referred couple is in, *they* are in the same position — they qualify by referring one more couple. Each link qualifies itself by extending the chain.
- This is the growth engine: every couple is motivated to recruit the next, and qualification spreads couple-to-couple.

> **Worked example:** Couple A refers Couple B → A is qualified. B now refers Couple C → B is qualified. C refers D → C is qualified. The chain grows the couple base while qualifying everyone in it.

**For the referral to count (anti-fraud, see §5):** the referred couple must be a *distinct, email-verified couple with a completed profile* (story + at least one photo) — not an empty shell. This stops a couple from spinning up a fake second "couple" just to qualify itself.

### Path B — Vote knockout (the open fallback)
- For couples who can't or don't want to refer someone: reach the **minimum vote threshold** during the qualification window (the original knockout).
- This path must always stay open, so the last couples in a chain — who may struggle to find someone new to refer — are never trapped.

### Path C — Founders' bonus (first launch cohort only, optional & capped)
- For a country's very first activation, the earliest **N** pre-registrants who have completed their profile may be auto-qualified as a thank-you to early believers.
- Strictly capped (e.g. first 50) and used only for the inaugural round, so it rewards pioneers without flooding the launch with auto-admits. Drop it for subsequent months.

## 4. Gates & Guardrails (the things that keep this from breaking)

| Risk | Guardrail |
|---|---|
| Country stalls with no sponsor | Defined fallback: company-funded starter prize after a deadline (Phase 1). |
| Waitlist floods with dead profiles | Qualification is *earned* (referral or votes), not given. Founders' bonus is capped. |
| Couples leak during the waiting gap | Keep pre-reg couples active: profile-building, story posts, referrals, a pre-launch "most anticipated" list. |
| Latecomers feel they start behind | Live round starts only after the qualification window closes — everyone enters together. |
| Referral fraud (fake couples) | Referred couple must be verified + have a complete profile before the referral counts; flag shared device/IP/payment fingerprints. |
| Spreading too thin across countries | Sponsor pipeline drives sequencing — activate a country only when its sponsor is locked. |

---

## 5. Country Sequencing Strategy

- **Launch one country first.** Pick the market with the strongest sponsor lead and the clearest legal path. Prove the entire loop end-to-end: pre-reg → sponsor → qualification (all three paths) → live → payout.
- **Country #2 runs the playbook, doesn't reinvent it.** Once the loop works, each new country is execution, not design.
- **Let the sponsor pipeline set the order, not market size.** A secured sponsor is the real unlock for each market. A large market with no sponsor is not ready; a smaller market with a committed sponsor is.
- **Each country needs its own legal check** before activation — prize-competition and payment/e-money rules differ by country and must be confirmed with a local specialist, then reflected in that country's published competition terms. *(This playbook is operational, not legal advice.)*

## 6. Social Media Playbook

Social is the amplifier for the referral loop and the daily heartbeat that drives couples' own audiences back to the platform.

### Accounts
- Start with **one main brand account** per platform (Instagram, Facebook, TikTok).
- As countries scale and need local language, spin up **per-country handles** (e.g. `@favoritecouple.de`) — but not before a country justifies it. Don't fragment attention early.

### Content tied to each phase
- **Phase 0 (pre-reg):** countdowns, "this country is filling up," waitlist milestones, calls to invite another couple (drives Path A referrals).
- **Phase 1 (sponsor reveal):** the prize announcement — a launch moment. Tag and celebrate the sponsor.
- **Phase 2 (qualification):** "qualification is open," "X couples qualified so far," "window closes in 3 days" (urgency).
- **Phase 3 (live):** **daily couple spotlights** (your single most important recurring post — it sends each couple's followers back to vote), leaderboard updates, growing-prize-tier unlocks, "voting closes soon."
- **Phase 4 (winner):** winner reveal + sponsor thank-you. This becomes proof material for the next sponsor.

### Cadence
- **Daily during a live competition** (couple spotlights are the backbone).
- A few times a week during pre-registration and qualification.
- Reshare couples' own daily posts — it costs you nothing, flatters the couple, and pulls in their audience.

### Loop reinforcement
- Every couple posts with a **branded hashtag** and their **referral code/link**. When the platform reshares, it amplifies that couple's recruitment — social distribution and the referral chain feed each other.

## 7. Per-Country Launch Checklist

A tickable sequence to run for each new country:

1. ☐ Open pre-registration for the country (always-on).
2. ☐ Reach a minimum waitlist size that makes a sponsor pitch credible.
3. ☐ Complete the country's legal check (prize + payment rules); finalize competition terms.
4. ☐ Secure a sponsor **or** trigger the company-funded starter-prize fallback.
5. ☐ Announce the prize / sponsor reveal across social.
6. ☐ Open the qualification window (referral + vote-knockout paths; founders' bonus only if first round).
7. ☐ Run daily/qualification-phase social content.
8. ☐ Close the qualification window; lock the qualified field.
9. ☐ Start the live competition; run daily couple spotlights and milestone posts.
10. ☐ Declare winner; verify identity; pay out.
11. ☐ Post winner + sponsor thank-you; capture it as proof for the next sponsor.
12. ☐ Roll into the next monthly round; carry forward couples and learnings.

---

## 8. Risks & Notes

- **Referral fraud** is the main new risk this model introduces — guard it with verification + complete-profile requirements before a referral counts, and fingerprint-cluster flagging (consistent with the build spec's anti-fraud section).
- **Keep the vote-knockout path always open** so chains can't trap their last members.
- **Privacy:** referral relationships are personal data — store and disclose them per GDPR in the country's privacy terms.
- This is an operational playbook, not legal advice. Confirm prize-competition and payment regulations per country before activating, and reflect them in each country's published terms.
