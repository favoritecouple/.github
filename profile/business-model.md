# Favorite — Business Model

*A popularity-contest platform built as a reusable **format**: register a contestant, tell its story, collect free votes, climb a tiered popularity ladder, funded by sponsors and advertising. **Free to enter, free to win** — money never decides the outcome. The format launches with pets and expands vertical by vertical.*

---

## 1. The Core Insight: It's a Format, Not One App

The product is a repeatable engine that works for any contestant people are proud enough to promote to their own network:

> Register a contestant -> tell its story -> collect free daily votes -> climb a tiered ladder (country -> regional -> continental -> global) -> funded by sponsors + ads -> finished entries become permanent evergreen story pages.

"Couples" was the original idea, but it is only one vertical — and, as it turns out, the hardest one to start with. The same engine runs pets, cars, gardens, and couples by swapping the contestant. So the strategy is: build the engine once, prove it in the easiest, most monetizable vertical, then template it across the others.

---

## 2. Vertical Strategy & Launch Order

Verticals are sequenced by **ease of launch and revenue value** — lightest legal/moderation load and fattest sponsor pipeline first, hardest last.

| Order | Vertical | Why this slot | Ad RPM (global, est.) | Sponsor pipeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Pets — Dogs** | Lightest legal load, huge supply, easy sponsors | ~$3 | Pet food, vets, insurers (excellent) |
| 1 | **Pets — Cats** | Same engine, second pet competition | ~$3 | Same as dogs |
| 2 | **Cars / builds** | Highest ad value, content-rich SEO | ~$5 | Parts, insurance, dealers (strong) |
| 3 | **Gardens** | Very low moderation, decent value | ~$3 | Nurseries, tools, home brands |
| 4 | **Couples** | Lowest ad value, heaviest moderation — last | ~$2 | Travel, jewelry (thinner) |

**Why pets first.** No privacy or breakup drama, no harassment-driven takedowns, no GDPR-on-intimate-relationships load — a pet has no reputation to protect. The sponsor pipeline is deep and obvious (pet food, vets, insurers), supply and sharing are effectively unlimited, and pet content is heavily searched (strong SEO). Crucially, nothing in the engine changes — pets is simply an easier instance of the same product. Dogs and Cats run as two parallel competitions.

**Why couples last.** Lowest ad RPM and the heaviest content/privacy/moderation burden (real, identifiable people; breakups; takedowns). Launch it only once the platform and moderation systems are mature.

**Deliberately excluded:** babies/children and any attractiveness/pageant framing — severe child-safety and exploitation risk that no revenue justifies. Contestants are either non-human or consenting adults presenting something they made or are.

**Revenue effect of the portfolio.** Multiple commercial-intent verticals raise the blended ad RPM to roughly $3–$3.5 versus ~$2 for couples-only — a ~50–75% lift per pageview — while multiplying the addressable audience, the evergreen-SEO page base, and the sponsor pool. See the financial model.

---

## 3. Users (per vertical)

**Voters** — register to vote, one free vote per competition per day, never pay.

**Contestants** — registered owners enter their dog, cat, car, garden, or couple, post a story and media, and compete. Entering and winning are free; contestants never pay to compete or improve standing.

---

## 4. Competitions

| Level | Frequency | Automatic advancement |
|---|---|---|
| Country | Monthly | — |
| Regional | Quarterly | Top 10 from each monthly country competition |
| Continental | Half-yearly | Top 10 from each quarterly regional competition |
| Global | Yearly | Top 10 from each half-yearly continental competition |

Each competition belongs to a vertical (e.g. "Germany — Favorite Dog, monthly"). Any contestant can join via a qualification (knockout) window, the referral path, or (first cohort only) the founders' bonus. Top 10 from the previous equivalent competition skip the knockout.

---

## 5. Voting

- **Free daily vote** — every registered voter gets one free vote per competition, per day. The only way votes are cast; there are no paid votes.
- **Free 5x boost** — every contestant gets one free 24-hour 5x boost per competition, activated whenever they choose. During an active boost, votes received count 5x. No purchasable boost.
- **Ranking** — total votes received (boost-window votes weighted 5x). **No payment affects the ranking or who wins.**

---

## 6. Prizes

A **fixed amount announced before each round**, funded by a sponsor and/or the company. Never a percentage of payments; nothing a participant pays affects who wins.

Prize-type ladder (easiest to operate -> hardest): (1) sponsor in-kind (the easiest first prize in every vertical), (2) company-purchased gift cards / vouchers, (3) travel / experience vouchers, (4) cash (last; requires payout rails). Growing-prize feel = company-set milestone tiers.

---

## 7. Revenue Streams

No revenue from voters; no payment affects the outcome.

1. **Sponsor prize funding** — covers the prize cost (deepest and easiest in pets and cars).
2. **Sponsor logo / ad listings** — brands paying for placement within a competition.
3. **Display advertising / AdSense** — served to the voter audience; value rises with the higher-intent verticals (cars, pets) and organic SEO traffic.
4. **Contestant subscriptions** — optional premium features (see §8).

---

## 8. Subscriptions — Outcome-Neutral by Design

Subscriptions must not let money buy a better chance of winning.

**Recommended (outcome-neutral):** verified badge, profile customization, analytics (who's voting, when), priority support, cosmetic flair.

**Handle with care (may influence visibility/votes):** more posts per day, improved visibility, social features. These are promotional, not mechanical (they add no votes), but "paying for visibility that helps you win" is the one line a regulator may scrutinize — keep them off the ranking surface and confirm with a solicitor before charging.

---

## 9. SEO & Evergreen Content (long-term traffic engine)

Each finished competition converts a contestant's entry into a **permanent "story" page** with a canonical URL that never changes, so link equity accumulates across rounds. Over time these compound into large long-tail organic traffic — higher-intent and higher-RPM than social bursts. Cars and pets are especially strong here (heavily searched, content-rich).

**This is a year-2/3 payoff**, not early revenue — SEO compounds over 12–24 months. **Critical quality rule:** search engines penalize thin, mass-generated pages. Value is unique substance per page, not page count — prompt contestants to write a *real* story with real media. The platform's job is to encourage genuine storytelling.

---

## 10. The Retention & Momentum Loop

- Non-winning contestants re-enter the next round, carrying their audience forward.
- Each round adds permanent story pages (SEO base) and new contestants via the referral chain.
- Each successful round and winner becomes proof for the next sponsor, building sponsor momentum across rounds, countries, and verticals.
- More contestants -> more pages and shares -> more traffic -> more contestants and more attractive to sponsors. Slow to start, then accelerates.

Land the rollout and early sponsors correctly and the loop carries growth — but it is a year-2/3 payoff, so early survival depends on per-round prize affordability and sponsor-first launches.

---

## 11. Legal Posture

Deliberately **free to enter and free to win**: voters never pay, contestants never pay to compete or win, no payment affects the outcome. Removing the participant "stake" moves the product toward a normal free-to-enter, ad-funded popularity contest. Pets/cars/gardens further reduce content/privacy risk versus couples.

Still required, not optional:
- **UK gambling-law solicitor sign-off before taking any money or launching**, including review of visibility-related subscription perks.
- **Free daily vote always free and ungated** — the genuine free-entry route.
- **Consumer / prize-promotion rules** (clear terms, honest prize advertising, transparent winner selection).
- **GDPR** (couples especially, but all personal data).
- **Ad-network content policies** (AdSense) require real moderation — moderation protects revenue, not only safety.

---

## 12. Launch Plan (MVP)

1. Build the engine **category-aware** (verticals are config, not rewrites).
2. Launch vertical = **Pets**, run as two competitions: **Dogs** and **Cats**.
3. One country, monthly competitions; contestants register, post a story, compete.
4. Voters register and get a free daily vote; every contestant gets its free 5x boost.
5. Fixed, pre-announced prize — sponsor in-kind first, company gift cards as fallback.
6. Revenue from sponsors, ads/AdSense, and subscriptions.
7. Fraud controls + moderation from day one.

Prove the pets loop in one country, then expand country-by-country within pets, then add **Cars**, then **Gardens**, then **Couples**.

---

## 13. Open Decisions

- Confirm pets as launch vertical (recommended) and whether to accelerate **Cars** ahead of Gardens, since cars is the highest-RPM vertical.
- One brand spanning all verticals vs separate branded properties on one engine (shapes domains, social accounts, SEO).
- Visibility-affecting subscription perks: offered or not, and how to keep ranking-neutral.
- Base prize sizing and vote thresholds; subscription tier contents and price.
- First launch country (drives the legal review).
