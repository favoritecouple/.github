# Favorite Couple — Business Model

*A social voting platform where couples share their story and compete to become their country's, region's, continent's, and the world's favorite couple.*

---

## 1. Concept

Couples sign up as a pair, post their story and photos/videos, and compete in a tiered series of popularity competitions. Anyone can register as a voter to support a favorite couple. Winning couples earn prizes. The platform grows through the couples themselves, who bring their own audiences.

---

## 2. Users

**Voters** — register to vote, receive one free vote per competition per day, and can buy extra votes to support a couple. Voters pay money but cannot win the prize.

**Couples** — register as a pair, post their story and content, and compete. Couples can win prizes but cannot buy votes.

---

## 3. Competitions

| Level | Frequency | Automatic advancement |
|---|---|---|
| Country | Monthly | — |
| Regional | Quarterly | Top 10 from each monthly country competition |
| Continental | Half-yearly | Top 10 from each quarterly regional competition |
| Global | Yearly | Top 10 from each half-yearly continental competition |

**Qualification (knockout) phase.** Any couple can attempt to join a competition by passing a knockout window (e.g. 14 days), collecting a minimum number of votes to qualify. Minimum thresholds scale with level (e.g. monthly = 10, regional = 100, increasing upward). Top 10 from the previous equivalent competition skip the knockout and advance automatically.

---

## 4. Voting

- **Free daily vote** — every voter gets one free vote per competition, per day.
- **Paid votes** — voters buy additional votes. 1 vote = €1, with lower per-vote pricing in bundles (10, 100, etc.).
- **Free 5× boost day** — each competing couple gets one free 24h window per round where all received votes are multiplied 5×.
- **Paid 5× boost** — each couple can buy one additional 24h 5× window per round. Price scales with competition level (lower in smaller rounds, higher in larger ones).

---

## 5. Prizes

The prize for each round is a fixed amount the company decides and announces **before** the round begins. It is funded from company revenue and/or by a sponsor.

**Fixed prize.** The company sets a prize figure per round that it can afford and commits to it publicly in advance.

**Tiered growing prize.** The announced prize can increase as the round hits milestones the company sets, e.g. "€500 base; €750 at 5,000 votes; €1,000 at 10,000 votes." Each increase is a commitment the company chooses to make.

**Sponsor prizes.** Sponsors can fund prizes directly — cash, a 7-day vacation (hotel + flights), product bundles, or a title.

---

## 6. Revenue Streams

1. **Paid votes** — voters buying extra votes (€1 each / cheaper in bundles).
2. **Paid 5× boosts** — couples buying their extra boost window per round.
3. **Couple premium features** — verified badge, better feed placement, profile analytics (who's voting and when), extra daily posts, custom profile design; recurring subscription.
4. **Sponsor prize funding** — sponsors paying to provide a round's prize.
5. **Sponsor logo / ad listings** — brands paying for placement and visibility within a competition.
6. **Display advertising** — ads served to the voter audience.

All revenue comes in as company income. The prize is a cost line the company controls and sets per round.

---

## 7. Cost Structure

- Prizes (set per round, funded from revenue or by sponsors)
- Payment processing fees
- Hosting / infrastructure
- Salaries and team
- Customer acquisition / marketing
- Content and social media production
- Moderation and support
- Fraud prevention and identity verification
- Legal and compliance
- Taxes

---

## 8. Growth Engine

- Each competing couple can publish one post (picture + text) per day to gain traction, turning every couple into a channel that brings its own audience.
- The platform runs its own Instagram, Facebook, and TikTok accounts, publishing couples and their stories to drive traffic back to the app.
- The "most-loved couple" premise is inherently shareable, driving organic growth.

---

## 9. Operations & Compliance

- **Free entry route** — the free daily vote provides a no-purchase way to participate.
- **Published round rules** — each round publishes the prize, how the winner is decided, the free entry route, and tie-breaking.
- **Winner payout** — identity verification and a payout method for winning couples.
- **Fraud controls** — anti-bot and fake-account detection; detection of couples voting through proxy accounts.
- **Privacy & moderation** — content takedown process, consent/ownership rules, harassment moderation, age verification.
- **Legal review** — prize-competition and financial rules confirmed per country before operating there. *(Structural model only; not legal advice — confirm with a local specialist.)*

---

## 10. Launch Plan (MVP)

1. One competition only: monthly, country-level.
2. Couples register, post a story, and compete.
3. Voters register, get a free daily vote, and can buy extra votes.
4. A modest, fixed, pre-announced prize (company- or sponsor-funded).
5. Built-in social sharing plus the platform's own social accounts.
6. Fraud controls and content moderation from day one.

Expand to the regional / continental / global ladder once couples reliably bring audiences and fraud is controlled in one country.

---

## 11. Open Decisions

- Whether paid votes move the leaderboard or only fund/grow the prize.
- Base prize sizing and the vote thresholds that increase it.
- Boost pricing per competition level.
- Couple premium tier contents and price.
