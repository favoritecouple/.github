# Favorite — Financial Model

*Multi-vertical platform. Free-to-enter, free-to-win: no revenue from voters, no paid votes/boosts. Income from sponsors, advertising/AdSense, and contestant subscriptions. Figures EUR/month unless stated; founder salary excluded and noted. Launch vertical = Pets (Dogs + Cats).*

> **All numbers are editable assumptions, not predictions.** The model is honest about a hard truth: this is **thin and slow early**, and profitability depends on **scale**, **sponsors**, and the **higher-RPM verticals** — not on early monetization.

---

## 1. Per-Vertical Ad Value (the reason sequencing matters)

Niche is the strongest RPM driver. Verticals are launched roughly highest-ease/value first, lowest last.

| Vertical | Global blended RPM (est.) | Sponsor pipeline | Notes |
|---|---:|---|---|
| Cars / builds | ~$5 | Strong (parts, insurance, dealers) | Highest ad value; content-rich SEO |
| Pets (dogs/cats) | ~$3 | Excellent (food, vets, insurers) | Launch vertical; huge supply |
| Gardens | ~$3 | Good (nurseries, tools) | Lowest moderation |
| Couples | ~$2 | Thinner (travel, jewelry) | Lowest value; launched last |
| **Portfolio blended** | **~$3–3.5** | — | ~50–75% higher RPM than couples-only |

RPMs are global-blended estimates; Tier-1-heavy traffic runs 40–120% higher, tier-3-heavy lower. Replace with real AdSense data within the first months.

## 4. Staged Vertical Ramp (where revenue compounds)

| Stage | Live verticals | Revenue character |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Pets (Dogs + Cats), 1 country | Sponsor-led; ads negligible; net slightly negative |
| 2 | Pets, multiple countries | Sponsors scale; ads start mattering; approaching break-even |
| 3 | + Cars | Highest-RPM vertical lifts blended ad revenue and adds a deep sponsor pool |
| 4 | + Gardens | Broader audience + SEO surface; incremental |
| 5 | + Couples | Audience breadth; lowest RPM, so additive not transformative |

Cars (Stage 3) is the biggest single revenue step-up — reason to consider accelerating it ahead of gardens.

---

## 5. Where Profitability Comes From

1. **Audience scale** — ad revenue only matters at tens of thousands of daily visitors; the four-vertical engine reaches that faster (more audiences, more SEO pages).
2. **Sponsors** — the largest near-term line and the prize funder; pets and cars have the deepest, warmest pools.
3. **Higher-RPM verticals** — adding cars lifts the blended RPM the most.
4. **Cheap (organic/referral) growth** — paid marketing vs organic is the difference between profitable and not.
5. **Evergreen SEO** — compounds over 12–24 months into high-RPM organic traffic (strongest in cars and pets).

## 7. Key Assumptions & Caveats

- Founder salary excluded; add as a fixed cost once drawn.
- RPMs are estimates; verify against real AdSense data early. Worldwide traffic drags RPM down; Tier-1 skew lifts it.
- Display ads earn almost nothing below tens of thousands of engaged voters.
- Sponsor income is assumed but gated per launch by securing one (see rollout playbook).
- Pageviews-per-visit is pivotal: social-share-and-bounce gives ~1–2 pv/visit and roughly halves ad revenue; deeper SEO sessions raise it.
- This model trades fast paid-vote revenue for legal simplicity — a deliberate choice with a longer road to profit, offset by the better-RPM vertical mix.
